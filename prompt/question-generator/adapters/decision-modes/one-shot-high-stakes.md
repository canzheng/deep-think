DECISION MODE — ONE-SHOT HIGH-STAKES

Use when:
- downside is large
- repetition is limited
- mistakes are costly
- reversal is difficult or expensive

Research behavior:
- raise the evidence threshold
- widen scenario coverage
- emphasize robustness, pre-mortems, and downside containment
- ask what must be true before acting
- privilege irreversible-risk analysis over elegance

Key questions:
- What error would be hardest to reverse?
- What must be true for the decision to be acceptable?
- What uncertainty can be reduced before action?
- What uncertainty must simply be accepted?
- What is the minimum robust action rather than the maximum aggressive action?

Action logic:
- robustness > optimization
- avoid ruin
- use kill criteria and explicit no-go thresholds

Monitoring style:
- milestone-based, not twitchy

Failure mode:
- analysis paralysis

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the action problem as high-cost, low-repeat, and difficult to reverse.

Boundary — Light
- Pulls in downside, robustness, and irreversible-risk considerations.

Structure — Modulating
- Adds action-relevant structure around error cost and fragility.

Scenarios — Primary
- Favors downside-heavy and robustness-focused branches over elegance.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions about what must be true before acting and what error is hardest to reverse.

Evidence Planning — Modulating
- Raises the evidence bar and emphasizes pre-mortems and disconfirming evidence.

Decision Logic — Primary
- Core stage: must define no-go thresholds, robustness, and kill criteria.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should indicate whether the preconditions for acting have been met or broken.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should be milestone-based, not twitchy.

Render — Modulating
- Decision outputs should explicitly show robustness, no-go conditions, and downside containment.
