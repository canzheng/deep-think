DECISION MODE — LATENT / NO IMMEDIATE COMMITMENT

Use when:
- the user is mainly framing the problem
- action is deferred
- preserving option value matters more than immediate choice

Research behavior:
- optimize for clarity, not premature action
- identify the future decision that this research is setting up
- distinguish "good to know" from "must know later"

Key questions:
- What future decision is this analysis preparing for?
- What must be known before that decision becomes live?
- What information can be gathered cheaply now?
- What uncertainty is irrelevant unless commitment approaches?

Action logic:
- preserve flexibility
- build the decision frame
- avoid fake precision

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the action problem as preparatory rather than immediately executable.

Boundary — Light
- Keeps scope focused on framing the future decision, not forcing action now.

Structure — Light
- Adds action-relevant framing only where it clarifies the later decision.

Scenarios — Modulating
- Favors scenarios that clarify what future decision may become live.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions about what will matter later and what can be learned cheaply now.

Evidence Planning — Modulating
- Lowers pressure for immediate proof while prioritizing cheap informative evidence.

Decision Logic — Primary
- Core stage: should define the future decision frame without premature commitment.

Signal Translation — Modulating
- Signals should indicate when the latent decision becomes active.

Monitoring — Modulating
- Monitoring should watch for transition from optional framing to live decision.

Render — Modulating
- Should make clear that the output is a decision frame, not an immediate commitment.
