DECISION MODE — OPTIONALITY / STAGED COMMITMENT

Use when:
- the user can buy information in stages
- the initial move can be small and reversible
- later commitment can depend on evidence

Research behavior:
- lower the evidence bar for the first reversible step
- define explicit stage gates
- identify the cheapest informative experiment
- ask what evidence unlocks scaling versus what kills the thesis

Key questions:
- What is the smallest informative action?
- What evidence would justify the next stage?
- What is the option cost?
- What are the kill criteria?
- What should be learned before scaling?

Action logic:
- cheap probes first
- preserve upside, cap downside
- escalate only on validation

Monitoring style:
- milestone and stage-gate based

Failure mode:
- endless small experiments with no scale discipline

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the action problem as stageable and partially reversible.

Boundary — Light
- Pulls in stage gates, probe design, and future commitment points.

Structure — Modulating
- Adds reversible versus irreversible moves and low-cost experiment structure.

Scenarios — Primary
- Favors branches tied to probe, scale, pause, and stop decisions.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions about what unlocks the next stage and what kills the thesis.

Evidence Planning — Modulating
- Ties evidence to explicit stage gates rather than one final proof threshold.

Decision Logic — Primary
- Core stage: must define probe, scale, kill, and learning-before-commitment logic.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should identify probe success, scaling triggers, and stop conditions.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should be milestone-based and stage-gate oriented.

Render — Modulating
- Outputs should make staged commitment and option value explicit.
