DECISION MODE — PORTFOLIO CONSTRUCTION

Use when:
- the choice is allocation across multiple opportunities
- diversification, concentration, and correlation matter
- the right question is marginal contribution, not standalone attractiveness

Research behavior:
- change unit of analysis from single idea to portfolio role
- identify common drivers and shared failure modes
- ask what this adds, replaces, hedges, or duplicates

Key questions:
- What role does this position play?
- What exposures does it share with the rest of the portfolio?
- Where is correlation hidden?
- What failure modes are duplicated?
- What is the marginal contribution to expected return and drawdown?

Action logic:
- standalone quality is insufficient
- evaluate fit, covariance, liquidity, and concentration

Monitoring style:
- portfolio-level and position-level

Failure mode:
- false diversification

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the action problem as allocation within a portfolio rather than standalone choice.

Boundary — Light
- Pulls in correlation, overlap, concentration, and marginal portfolio role.

Structure — Modulating
- Adds common drivers, hidden correlations, and shared failure modes to the structure.

Scenarios — Primary
- Favors branches evaluated by marginal portfolio effect, not standalone attractiveness.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions about role, overlap, duplication, and marginal contribution.

Evidence Planning — Modulating
- Prefers evidence that distinguishes standalone quality from portfolio fit.

Decision Logic — Primary
- Core stage: must define sizing, diversification, hedging, and substitution logic.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should indicate when to add, trim, hedge, or reduce overlap.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should include both position-level and portfolio-level stress signals.

Render — Modulating
- Decision and worksheet outputs should show portfolio role and sizing logic explicitly.
