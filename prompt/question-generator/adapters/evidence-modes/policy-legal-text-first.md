EVIDENCE MODE — POLICY / LEGAL-TEXT-FIRST

Prioritize:
- bill text
- rule text
- court opinions
- implementation guidance
- delegated authority
- procedural timelines

Strengths:
- precise on authority and enforceability

Weaknesses:
- weak on politics or enforcement intensity by itself

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Selects a legal-text-first proof style.

Boundary — Light
- Slightly narrows scope toward what authority and procedure can actually support.

Structure — Modulating
- Encourages mechanism claims anchored in authority, delegation, procedure, and implementation.

Scenarios — Modulating
- Prefers branches driven by actual implementation paths, legal authority, and judicial outcomes.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions about what authority exists, what is discretionary, and what implementation path is real.

Evidence Planning — Primary
- Sets source hierarchy around law, regulation, guidance, procedure, and court decisions.

Decision Logic — Modulating
- Raises the evidence bar when legal authority is necessary before action.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Favors bill text, rule text, opinions, guidance, and delegated-authority signals.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should track procedural milestones, litigation, implementation guidance, and enforcement signals.

Render — Light
- Usually affects how explicitly authority and enforceability are shown.
