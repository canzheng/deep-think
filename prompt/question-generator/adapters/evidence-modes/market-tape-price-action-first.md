EVIDENCE MODE — MARKET-TAPE / PRICE-ACTION-FIRST

Prioritize:
- price action
- relative moves
- volatility
- options skew
- flows
- spreads
- revisions
- dispersion

Strengths:
- fast
- useful for expectation mapping and timing

Weaknesses:
- noisy
- technical distortion risk

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Selects a tape-first proof style oriented around expectation mapping.

Boundary — Light
- Slightly narrows attention toward what should show up in market behavior.

Structure — Modulating
- Encourages structure that stays close to tradable expectation shifts and timing.

Scenarios — Modulating
- Prefers scenarios that can be expressed through revisions, spreads, volatility, and price response.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions like what will show up in revisions, spreads, or relative price action.

Evidence Planning — Primary
- Sets freshness bias, price-action priority, and conflict rules for noisy signals.

Decision Logic — Modulating
- Can justify action on tape confirmation even when direct evidence is incomplete.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Favors price, vol, flows, revisions, skew, and relative-move signals.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should track confirmation, rejection, persistence, and crowding rather than one-off prints.

Render — Light
- Usually only changes how explicitly expectation mapping is shown.
