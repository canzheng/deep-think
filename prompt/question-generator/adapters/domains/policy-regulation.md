## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the problem as a policy, legal, or implementation system.

Boundary — Primary
- Pulls in agencies, courts, implementation chains, appropriations, and coalition politics.

Structure — Primary
- Makes legal authority, enforcement capacity, coalition support, and implementation mechanics central.

Scenarios — Primary
- Favors pass/fail, enforcement, litigation, implementation delay, and coalition-fracture branches.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors authority, enforceability, discretion, implementation, and judicial-risk questions.

Evidence Planning — Primary
- Prefers legal text, agency guidance, procedural timing, litigation, and enforcement evidence.

Decision Logic — Modulating
- Shapes what action means: intervention feasibility, compliance planning, timing, and leverage points.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should emphasize bill text changes, rulemaking, litigation, appropriations, and compliance behavior.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should track implementation chain progress, enforcement intensity, and coalition shifts.

Render — Light
- Mainly affects domain vocabulary and examples.
