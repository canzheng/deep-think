## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the problem as a market or capital-allocation problem shaped by expectations and price.

Boundary — Primary
- Pulls in customers, management, regulation, valuation regime, positioning, and capital flows.

Structure — Primary
- Makes expectations, catalysts, liquidity, and capital flows structural forces, not side notes.

Scenarios — Primary
- Favors rerating, derating, execution miss, demand durability, and catalyst unlock branches.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors expectation-versus-reality, catalyst timing, crowding, and valuation questions.

Evidence Planning — Primary
- Prefers filings, guidance, revisions, tape, spreads, and operating confirmation.

Decision Logic — Modulating
- Shapes what action means: sizing, timing, hedge, add, trim, or avoid.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should emphasize revisions, spreads, relative price action, positioning, and operating confirmation.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should track expectation shifts, crowding, catalysts, and correlation stress.

Render — Light
- Mainly affects domain vocabulary and examples, not structure by itself.
