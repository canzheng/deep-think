## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the problem as a company, product, or go-to-market system.

Boundary — Primary
- Pulls in buyer, user, workflow, channel, ecosystem, and execution context.

Structure — Primary
- Makes PMF, switching cost, trust, onboarding friction, and channel behavior central structural elements.

Scenarios — Primary
- Favors adoption, stall, competitor displacement, channel leverage, and execution-miss branches.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions on buyer behavior, distribution, adoption bottlenecks, pricing, and retention quality.

Evidence Planning — Primary
- Prefers operating metrics, customer behavior, workflow evidence, and field friction evidence.

Decision Logic — Modulating
- Shapes what action means: resource allocation, sequencing, investment, pricing, or GTM change.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should emphasize churn, activation, win rates, adoption depth, and time-to-value.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should track segment behavior, usage quality, feature adoption, and sales-cycle change.

Render — Light
- Mainly affects examples and operational vocabulary.
