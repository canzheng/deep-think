UNCERTAINTY MODE — ADVERSARIAL / DECEPTION DOMINATED

Research behavior:
- prioritize revealed behavior over rhetoric
- test for signaling games
- discount cheap talk

Risk:
- excessive skepticism

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the problem as one where actors may manipulate perception.

Boundary — Light
- Can widen scope toward signaling channels, countermoves, and deception incentives.

Structure — Primary
- Forces the stage to distinguish rhetoric from costly signals and capability from narrative.

Scenarios — Primary
- Favors branches that depend on revealed behavior and strategic response, not cheap talk.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions about what cannot be cheaply faked and who benefits from belief error.

Evidence Planning — Primary
- Requires skepticism, conflict handling, and revealed-behavior preference.

Decision Logic — Primary
- Pushes action logic toward waiting for costly signals and avoiding rhetoric-driven overreaction.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should emphasize costly commitments and durable behavior over statements.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should downgrade rhetoric and watch for strategic behavior patterns.

Render — Modulating
- Should make deception risk and skepticism level explicit.
