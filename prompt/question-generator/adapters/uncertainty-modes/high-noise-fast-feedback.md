UNCERTAINTY MODE — HIGH-NOISE / FAST-FEEDBACK

Research behavior:
- filter aggressively
- require persistence or signal combinations
- avoid updating on every twitch

Risk:
- whipsaw from over-updating

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the problem as one with abundant but noisy short-cycle signals.

Boundary — Light
- Usually does not widen ontology much, but does narrow attention toward confirmation logic.

Structure — Primary
- Forces the stage to distinguish diagnostic structure from visible but low-value noise.

Scenarios — Primary
- Prefers disciplined branching without overreacting to every short-term move.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions about confirmation, persistence, and combinations of signals.

Evidence Planning — Primary
- Requires confirmation rules, noise penalties, and pattern-over-print logic.

Decision Logic — Primary
- Pushes action logic toward waiting for persistence and avoiding twitchy reactivity.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should require confirmation and persistence rather than isolated datapoints.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should be frequent but pattern-based, not hyper-reactive.

Render — Modulating
- Should make the confirmation standard explicit.
