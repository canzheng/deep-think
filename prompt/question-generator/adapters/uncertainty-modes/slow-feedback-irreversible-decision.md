UNCERTAINTY MODE — SLOW-FEEDBACK / IRREVERSIBLE-DECISION

Research behavior:
- emphasize pre-mortems
- stage decisions where possible
- seek disconfirming evidence early
- separate what must be known before acting from what can be learned later

Risk:
- excessive caution or delayed action

## Stage Relevance

Routing — Primary
- Classifies the problem as one where learning is slow and errors may be hard to reverse.

Boundary — Light
- Can widen scope toward reversibility, path dependence, and pre-decision kill criteria.

Structure — Primary
- Forces the stage to identify what matters before commitment and what can wait.

Scenarios — Primary
- Favors robust downside-aware branches rather than fragile fine-grained distinctions.

Question Generation — Primary
- Favors questions about what must be known before irreversible action.

Evidence Planning — Primary
- Requires pre-mortem evidence, disconfirming evidence, and clear distinction between before-action and after-action learning.

Decision Logic — Primary
- Strongly raises the bar for action and favors staging, reversibility, and kill criteria.

Signal Translation — Primary
- Signals should identify disconfirming milestones, stage gates, and stop-loss logic.

Monitoring — Primary
- Monitoring should be milestone-based and tied to irreversible-decision checkpoints.

Render — Modulating
- Should explicitly separate reducible uncertainty from uncertainty that must be borne.
